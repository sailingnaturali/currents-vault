Sechelt Rapids (Skookumchuck Narrows), at the entrance to Sechelt Inlet — one of
the world's fastest tidal rapids, about 16.5 knots on the flood and 16 on the
ebb on large tides. Transit only at slack; prefer high-water slack, routing west
of Boom Islet and giving Roland Point a wide berth. Standing waves, whirlpools,
and whitecaps build from the current alone, even in a flat calm.
