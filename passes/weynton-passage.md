Weynton Passage runs northwest from the junction of Johnstone and Broughton
Straits into Blackfish Sound and the east end of Cormorant Channel — the route
toward the Broughton Archipelago. Port McNeill, Alert Bay, and Sointula are
reached along Broughton Strait or through Cormorant Channel instead, not by
transiting Weynton. The whole passage lies inside Cormorant Channel Marine
Provincial Park.

Flood sets south (about 220 true), ebb north (about 040). A northbound boat
leaving Johnstone Strait for Blackfish Sound therefore carries a fair tide on the
ebb, southbound on the flood. The fairway is deep and never narrower than
0.7 mile, so the hazard is the water's behaviour rather than the channel's
dimensions — the dangers cluster along the edges.

Source confidence: high — CHS Sailing Directions PAC202 (2023/05) for all
geography, the 6-knot maximum, sets, and every named rock and reef. No source
states Weynton requires slack, so none is claimed here. PAC202's strong
wind-against-ebb cautions attach to Race Passage, Current Passage, Earl Ledge,
and Chatham Point well to the southeast — not to Weynton; likewise Robson Bight,
about 15 nm southeast, whose entry prohibition does not apply here.
