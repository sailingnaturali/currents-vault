  - state: any

An open-water reference in the central reach of Johnstone Strait, about 1.5 nm
west of Port Neville. Flood sets 100 true, ebb 280 true.

The strait is normally worked westbound with the ebb; eastbound against it can be
slow, and on small tides there may be no surface flood to wait for. The defining
hazard is the summer westerly, which typically fills in late morning, builds
through the afternoon, and dies near dusk — which is why the standard tactic is
to be underway at first light and anchored by early afternoon. Summer fog
commonly forms overnight and burns off through mid-morning, which can collide
with that early start.

Station note: CHS publishes no clean slack here. Instead of slack events this
station carries weak-and-variable periods, and often no flood maximum at all —
the data itself reflects the residual westward flow. Treat the quiet window as a
weak-and-variable stretch rather than a true reversal.

Source confidence: high for current behaviour, the Kelsey Bay–Port Neville rip
caution, traffic, and Robson Bight — all CHS Sailing Directions PAC202 (2023/07).
Lower for the rest: the "about 30 knots by late afternoon" sea-breeze figure is
CHS's for Queen Charlotte Strait, which funnels into the west end of Johnstone,
not a Johnstone figure; the afternoon wind strengths, named sea-state hotspots,
and the August–September fog pattern rest on cruising press. PAC202 publishes no
peak current figure for this station — daily rates are in Tide and Current Tables
Vol. 6. The drifting-log risk is inferred from the booming grounds and tows CHS
documents, not from a debris caution specific to the central strait.
