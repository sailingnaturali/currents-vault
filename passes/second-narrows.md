Second Narrows lies about 4.5 miles east of First Narrows, the pinch point
between Vancouver Harbour and the eastern arm of Burrard Inlet toward Port Moody
and Indian Arm. Flood sets 090 true, ebb 270 true.

An active commercial gate with grain, bulk, and tanker terminals on both sides.
Deep-sea traffic is scheduled into slack-water operational periods; small craft
are expected to stay clear and time their own passage near slack. Approaching
from the west, surface current is an unreliable guide to the water column in
late spring and summer — strong easterly bottom currents can run near Neptune
Bulk Terminals while the surface reads slack.
