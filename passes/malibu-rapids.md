hazards:
  - state: any
    text: >-
      Transit at or very near slack only. The channel is a narrow, blind
      S-shaped dogleg — roughly 30 m at its tightest — so announce the transit
      and give way to a vessel already committed. Slack is brief; a few minutes
      either side is the practical window.
  - state: flood
    text: >-
      The flood pours in from Queens Reach with overfalls and standing waves and
      sets hard across the dogleg; a boat arriving early is pushed toward the
      rock walls.
  - state: ebb
    text: >-
      The ebb drains Princess Louisa Inlet through the same narrows with
      overfalls off the entrance.

Reversing tidal rapids at the entrance to Princess Louisa Inlet, off the head of
Queens Reach in Jervis Inlet. There is no CHS current station here — slack is
derived from Point Atkinson high/low water (HW + 25 min / LW + 35 min, the
cruising-community consensus), so this gate carries slack timing only and no
predicted current speed or set.

Streams are reported to reach 8 to 9 knots at springs. Source confidence: medium
— slack-offset and peak-speed figures are cruising-community and secondary
references (Waggoner, Points North seminar), not a primary CHS current table
(none is published for this pass).
