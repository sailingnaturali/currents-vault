Blackney Passage is entered west of Cracroft Point and runs north between Hanson
and Parson Islands into Blackfish Sound, with Baronet Passage branching east just
north of Cracroft Point. Flood sets south (about 180 true), ebb north (about
355). A working commercial channel rather than a backwater, and a slack-water
transit.

Weynton Passage is a separate pass west of Hanson Island with its own station —
several sources blur the two, and the Bell Rocks circulation belongs to the
Blackney–Baronet junction specifically.

Source confidence: high — CHS Sailing Directions PAC202 (2023/07) for geography,
the 5-knot streams, the Cracroft Point races, and the Bell Rocks circulation;
DFO Pacific Marine Science Report 81-22 for the Seymour Narrows slack offsets.
Neither flood nor ebb is documented as the stronger, so neither is claimed. Peak
speed is contested — PAC202 gives 5 knots, a working ship's captain reports 4,
and an unsourced forum claim of 7 is discarded. PAC202 says tide races, never
whirlpools; the whirlpool claim off Cracroft Point comes only from paddler
forums and is not recorded here. No source documents wind-against-tide within
the passage itself.
