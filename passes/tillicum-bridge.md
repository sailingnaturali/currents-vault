The Gorge Waterway runs northwest from Victoria Harbour to Portage Inlet, and
Tillicum Bridge sits over its tightest constriction. Flood sets about 290 true
(inbound, toward Portage Inlet), ebb about 110 true.

This is not a cruising route. The bridges — the Selkirk Trestle above all —
exclude any masted or deep-draft vessel regardless of the state of the tide; the
water belongs to kayaks, canoes, rowing shells, dragon boats, and small harbour
ferries. Slack is also harder to time here than the current station alone
suggests: the tidal range in Portage Inlet is about half Victoria's, time offsets
from Victoria vary widely, and a long high-water stand precedes a small drop to
the next low.

Source confidence: clearances, the 6-knot maximum, and the Portage Inlet tidal
anomaly are CHS Sailing Directions PAC201 (2024/05). The maximum rate is
contested — local paddling and tourism sources variously claim 9 to 12 knots, and
one reports a single observed 8.5-knot ebb. Carry the CHS figure and treat the
higher numbers as unverified. CHS installed a current meter on the bridge in 2024
(station 07129), so published rates may yet be revised.
